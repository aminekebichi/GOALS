# Individual Reflection — Nicholas Annunziata
**CS7180 — Vibe Coding | GOALS Project**

---

Reflecting on the GOALS project, the most significant takeaway for me was the total transformation of my technical workflow through agentic AI. While I have considerable experience in full-stack development, this was my first time utilizing an assistant that was so deeply integrated into the entire lifecycle of a product. From managing our GitHub project board directly through the terminal to enforcing quality control with custom hooks, the speed and accuracy of the development loop were remarkable. It forced me to rethink my fundamental approach to project management and code stability.

On the technical side, resolving the Prisma binary target issue was a major learning point. Amine and I encountered a scenario where the application built perfectly in our CI environment but consistently failed in production with 500 errors. We had to dive deep into the Vercel logs to discover that the binary compiled on Ubuntu during the build process did not match the Amazon Linux runtime used by Vercel. Adding the specific binary targets to our Prisma schema was a simple fix, but identifying it taught me a valuable lesson about the hidden complexities of serverless deployments.

I was personally responsible for the clustering and classification stages of our machine learning pipeline. As someone who was initially unfamiliar with the intricacies of Premier League football, implementing K-Means to identify four distinct player archetypes was a rewarding way to learn about the league through raw data. This phase required a careful balancing of silhouette scores and practical interpretability to ensure our findings were meaningful in a sporting context. When it came to predicting match outcomes, achieving 55.6 percent accuracy with a Logistic Regression model felt like a genuine victory. This was a significant improvement over the 44 percent baseline, especially given the inherent randomness of professional sports. Working through these notebooks helped me appreciate the necessity of a strict temporal split to avoid any form of data leakage.

Another highlight of the project was our use of the GitHub MCP server to manage our agile process. Amine and I were able to handle our entire project board, including binary sprint milestones and over fifteen individual issues, without ever needing to open a web browser. This level of efficiency allowed us to stay focused on the code rather than administrative overhead. It also ensured that our project board remained a live and accurate reflection of our progress at all times.

Collaborating with Amine was a smooth process due to our clear division of responsibilities and our disciplined use of separate feature branches. We did face some challenges ensuring that the outputs from my classification notebooks integrated seamlessly with his seeding scripts for the web application. These moments highlighted the importance of well-defined data contracts between the machine learning pipeline and the application layer. In conclusion, I am incredibly proud of the deployed system we built and the professional growth I experienced throughout the semester.
